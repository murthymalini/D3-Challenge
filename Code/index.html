Census Data and Health Risks

Census Correlations Discovered Between Health Risks and Demographics

There is a clear correlation between poverty and healthcare, with the richest and poorest states at opposite corners of scatter plot.

There is a probable correlation between and age and healthcare coverage. The younger states do tend to have more lacking healthcare. However, with the median age of so many states being in the mid-30s, this is actually a small sample of different ages.

There is only a small correlation between poverty and obesity. The increase in obesity is slight as poverty increases, and obesity rates are in a very small range of percentages so it is hard to tell.

There is no visible correlation between age and obesity. The scatter plot is just a circular blob and at any given median age you can find states with very different obesity rates.
